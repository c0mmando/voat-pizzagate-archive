think- 2019-01-29T10:41:33

I would recommend giving money to your local women's shelter or homeless shelter.

Trafficking and abuse victims often get victimised again in adulthood, and are also far more likely to become homeless (a significant number of homeless people suffer from PTSD).

You can also ring them and ask what kind of goods they would be happy to receive as donations. Often they are happy to get (used) clothes and children's toys. Sometimes it's something seeemingly 'small' that can help tremendously - like buying proper clothes for a woman that needs them for a job interview, or providing a deposit so that someone will be able to rent a flat. This is the kind of help that can help changing someones life.

You can phone and ask if they have women/kids/men who have these special kind of needs - often the shelters are not able to cover costs like this from the funds they have.

The only nationwide charity I can recommend is SNAP - Survivors Network of those Abused by Priests.

Their website is: http://www.snapnetwork.org

They have done great, great work over the years to raise awareness about sexual abuse, they advocate for survivors, and are also lobbying for the abolishment of statutes of limitation.

The Survivors Network of those Abused by Priests, known as SNAP, established in 1989, is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization support group of survivors of clergy sexual abuse and their supporters in the United States.

SNAP's mission is to protect those who are vulnerable to child sexual abuse, to heal those who have been wounded, and to prevent future abuse. It exposes predators and those who shield them, helps members share stories so they are empowered, and educates communities about the impact of abuse.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Survivors_Network_of_those_Abused_by_Priests
