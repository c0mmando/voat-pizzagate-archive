Morning Star Boys Ranch

Morning Star Boys Ranch is a group home for boys located in Spokane, Washington.

Many associate the term "morning star" with Lucifer , although the term has also been used to represent Jesus in the Bible, Phosphorus in Greek mythology, and others.

19 sexual abuse lawsuits were filed against Morning Star Boys Ranch , starting in 2005. Only one went to trial. The litigation lasted seven years and damaged Morning Star's reputation . In 2012, the Seattle Times reported that the number of unresolved claims against Morning Star Boys Ranch was up to more than two dozen, and that these claims were being settled outside of court.

The allegations named Father Joe Weitensteiner, who began his career with Morning Star Boys Ranch in 1960 as a counselor and was promoted to director in 1966 after becoming an ordained priest. These allegations were dismissed, and he was reinstated as a priest by the Diocese . He now works for the Morning Star Foundation , whose major beneficiary is Morning Star Boys Ranch.

The allegations also named defrocked priest and pedophile Patrick O'Donnell, along with several counselors. Instead of handing O'Donnell over to law enforcement, after admitting to molesting "dozens of teenagers boys over three decades", the Catholic church tried to treat his pedophilia. When that failed, he was defrocked but never faced any jail time for his crimes. As of 2008, he was "free to roam" and living in La Conner, Washington .

One particularly disturbing detail of some allegations is that some of the abuse occurred on overnight stays outside of Morning Star Boys Ranch:

  • Weitensteiner recalled taking Putnam and another boy named Jeff Benz on an overnight trip on his boat on Lake Coeur d'Alene...

  • In 1996, Morning Star failed to properly report a resident’s complaint that he had been fondled by a man during an overnight stay at the man’s home. The boy’s social worker and probation officer did not approve the visit, and Morning Star had not conducted a background check on the man.

Morning Star officials...acknowledged recently that visitors were not required to register their names in ranch log books.

Morning Star Boys Ranch is still currently in operation.

Jim West

In 2005, Spokane's then-mayor Jim West was accused of molesting two boys while West was a Spokane sheriff's deputy and a boy scout leader. The other leader of West's troop, David Hahn, committed suicide in 1981 after being told to reisign or risk facing a criminal investigation .

West was on the board of directors for Morning Star Boys Ranch for 12 years . He resigned in 2005, after the accusations of pedophilia were brought to light.
