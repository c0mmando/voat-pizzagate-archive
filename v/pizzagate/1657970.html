Ya'll is incorrect; it's a mistake a lot of people make due to 'll being otherwise universal (it's used to replace "will", with the apostrophe replacing the "wi"). The mistake certainly could be more common in some places than others, but the "aw" sound only occurs in "all", not in "ya", which -- when used as slang for "you", anyway -- is pronounced "yuh", not "yaw". So "y'all" is a contraction of "you all" or "ya all", but in both cases, it's only the "a" in "all" which the apostrophe can replace. If the apostrophe replaced the "a" in "ya", "ya'll" would then be pronounced "yull". Nobody says it that way; it's "yawl".
