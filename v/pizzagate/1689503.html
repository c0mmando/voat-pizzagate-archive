Also, understanding the difference between inductive and deductive reasoning which I just learned. It's kind of something you understand when you see it, but don't have the terms for it.

Deductive Reasoning is stronger way of arguing. Deductive reasoning lets you come to a truthful conclusion IF the the premises are true.

Wikis allows contributors to edit pages True. Wikipedia is a Wiki. True. Therefore, we can say this conclusion is true : Wikipedia allows contributors to edit pages.

Inductive Reasoning is where the premise infers the conclusion is true, but the conclusion is not necessarily true, the conclusion does not have to follow.

Inductive Reasoning Every swan ever seen by an Englishman has been white.
Therefore every swan is white.

For awhile the premise was true and the conclusion seemed true since it was based on empircal evidence. However, it was not true as they discovered a species of swan in Australia that was black.

There is a ton of inductive reasoning going on this board and I'm not sure that people realize they are making an argument where their conclusion may or may not be true.

Here's our Premise. Wikileaks released a document showing that pedophiles often use spirals or butterflies as symbols to identify each other

Let's add another premise Sally is wearing a butterfly necklace and spiral earings.

Here's our Conclusion, Therefore we can say it's proven that Sally is a pedophile.

This is very weak argument. We don't know if Sally knows about the symbols at all. And all kinds of people wear butterfly or spiral jewelry are not pedophiles.

Pizzagate was built and a massive leap of logic based on inductive reasoning in the Podesta emails. Every bit of the "code" is inductive reasoning. Once you grasp this concept and the difference between deductive and inductive, I'm sure you can identify a lot of cases of inductive reasoning in the emails.
