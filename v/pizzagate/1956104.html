Peripheral intravenous lines

Preemie with scalp IV Peripheral intravenous (PIV) lines are catheters placed in veins in the hands, feet, or scalp. PIV lines can be inserted and left in place for a few days, and are generally used for delivery of nutrition or medication, and hydrating fluids. These lines are not threaded as deep into the body and usually last for hours to a few days; they are not the best option for babies who may need longer term IV therapy.
