A Reuters story today contains suspicious explanations of how two large ships ran into each other near the south coast of Japan, killing seven crew members of the US Navy ship. [ https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-navy-asia-exclusive-idUSKBN19H13C ].

"In clear weather south of Tokyo Bay in the early hours of June 17" the USS Fitzgerald collided with an ACX Crystal container ship. According to the Philippine ship's captain, the USS Fitzgerald failed to heed warnings of flashing lights before the container ship veered right to try to avoid it, but hit it anyway.

The Japan Coast Guard wasn't notified of the accident until about an hour after it happened.

"Shipping data in Thomson Reuters Eikon shows that the ACX Crystal, chartered by Japan's Nippon Yusen KK (9101.T), made a complete U-turn between 12:58 a.m. and 2:46 a.m." See YouTube video of ship's course: [ https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m1b58yelh_c ].

"The incident has spurred six investigations, including two internal hearings by the U.S. Navy and a probe by the United States Coast Guard (USCG) on behalf of the National Transportation Safety Board. The Japan Transport Safety Board, the JCG and the Philippines government are also conducting separate investigations."

"ACX Crystal has been owned by Olympic Steamship Co SA, Panama (a subsidiary of Japanese shipowner Sunford Shipping Ltd) and managed by Sea Quest Ship Management Inc of Bacoor, Philippines since 2008.[1][3] The ship has been bareboat-chartered to Sinbanali Shipping Inc of Manila since 2014,[3] but is reported by NYK Line on June 17 as being actually owned by Dainichi-Invest Corporation, a Japanese ship investment firm.[5] ACX Crystal is a mid-size container ship employed by ACX ('Asia Container Express'), the intra-Asia container shipping trade subsidiary of NYK Line, the global shipping and freight logistics division of the Japanese conglomerate Mitsubishi. In June 2017, ACX Crystal was employed by NYK on its 'PX1' (Phoenix 1) intra-Asia trade line linking Japan, Vietnam and Thailand ports." [ https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MV_ACX_Crystal ].
