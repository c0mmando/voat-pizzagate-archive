The statistics are too easy for skeptics to attack. The 8 million figure (and other similar figures) are based on reports, i.e. number of reports of missing children made each year in the U.S. and so on. These statistics don't explain how many children are never found, or how many children have been missing for X amount of time that are still not found, etc. Then you can find different sources giving different statistics on how many children are found, and though the numbers usually don't add up (and especially since there are multiple organizations all keeping separate statistics), for the general public they're not going to notice these finer issues and will instead just grab the first source that tells them that the vast majority of the reports are resolved with the children being safely returned home.

A much more important focus point is that it is nearly impossible to get the truly important data, i.e. being able to look up how many children have been missing for X amount of time, currently never found. This information isn't available, and that it's not available is rather concerning in itself.

A few weeks ago I did attempt to find this information, and instead what I found was several sources all with conflicting claims/information/numbers, along with estimations that were downright alarming.

http://www.slate.com/articles/news_and_politics/explainer/2007/01/800000_missing_kids_really.html

a 1997 study estimated that only 5 percent of nonfamily abductions (in which a nonfamily member detains a child using force for more than an hour) get reported to police.

What in the actual fuck?

In this source: http://www.missingkids.com/KeyFacts

It is claimed that

841 children have been successfully recovered as a result of the AMBER Alert program

as of 2015. That is less than 900 children over the course of more than a decade.

Yet this source: http://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-missing-children-idUSBRE83P14020120426

claims that

More than 99 percent of children reported missing in America in recent years have come home alive

And just so on and so forth the longer I searched. Nothing adds up right.

So rather than using statistics that are easy to pick apart depending on which of the many conflicting reports/sources that people use, it is far more disconcerting that there are so many conflicting reports/sources in which the numbers are not adding up.

At best it's a case of severe negligence over a very serious matter, and at worst it is a case of deliberate smoke and mirrors.
