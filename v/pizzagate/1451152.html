I wont be at my laptop for a few days, but anyone who know s about hex editing: Download the video as an mp4 (highest quality version), then get http://www.sweetscape.com/010editor/ there is a free 30day trial of it (they have a lot of various templates for different file structures including mp4).

Open the vid in the editor and look for a subsection called "mdat" this is the raw video (there may be more than one mdat). move the data from the mdat (s) and save in an empty file. You can then use the raw video data for putting into audacity or something. There is another program that takes anyfile and turns it's (byte?) structure into a visible sequence http://dsplab.diei.unipg.it/software/pyuv_raw_video_sequence_player

you can do this with plain audio too, though I'm not too keen on audio file structures.

if you put the raw data in audacity. goto file>import> rawdata i think and when it asks what kinda bytes you want to load it as select ulaw (i think everything else can be at the default.)
