Wikipedia points out the differences, right from the start, in their descriptions of these psychiatric disorders :

Pedophilia - This article is about the sexual preference toward prepubescent children. It is not to be confused with hebephilia or ephebophilia. Pedophilia or paedophilia is a psychiatric disorder in which an adult or older adolescent experiences a primary or exclusive sexual attraction to prepubescentchildren .

Hebephilia - Hebephilia is the strong, persistent sexual interest by adults in pubescent (early adolescent) children ..., typically ages 11–14. Hebephilia differs from pedophilia...

Ephebophilia is the primary or exclusive adult sexual interest in mid-to-late adolescents , generally ages 15 to 19. ... It is one of a number of sexual preferences across age groups subsumed under the technical term chronophilia . Ephebophilia strictly denotes the preference for mid-to-late adolescent sexual partners, not the mere presence of some level of sexual attraction.

Chronophilia The term chronophilia was used by John Money to describe a form of paraphilia in which an individual experiences sexual attraction limited to individuals of particular age ranges. ... Pedohebephilia refers to an expansion and reclassification of pedophilia and hebephilia with subgroups, proposed during the development of the DSM-5 (DSM-5 a topic in itself, but not necessarily here).
