Probably pure coincidence, and only barely related to pizzagate, but I was stunned to see Politics & Prose (bookstore near/related to Comet Ping Pong) mentioned in Thomas Pynchon's latest novel ( pizzagate )

submitted 2016-12-06T03:23:39 by szyrk

I've been a huge Thomas Pynchon fan for years. The more I looked into pizzagate, the more I was reminded of his work. So, knowing that his latest novel, Bleeding Edge (2013), has to do with the internet, 9/11, government conspiracies, etc., I decided to read it, figuring it would be comforting to read something quasi-similar but purely fictional. Then, tonight, on page 106, I read this:

"Listen, a suggestion, you're from D.C., try the self-help section Politics & Prose..."

Again, I have no reason to believe this is anything but coincidence. It looks like a fairly high-profile bookstore, so it shouldn't be surprising to see it mentioned. Still, rightly or wrongly, I feel compelled to post about it. If nothing else, let this serve as a book recommendation; it's been a welcome respite from reality while still being weirdly relevant and giving me a weird kind of perspective on pizzagate etc.

"Bleeding Edge is a novel by American author Thomas Pynchon, published by Penguin Press on September 17, 2013.The novel is a detective story, with its major themes being the September 11 attacks in New York City and the transformation of the world by the Internet." https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bleeding_Edge

"Pynchon’s latest novel, Bleeding Edge, does for 9/11 what Gravity’s Rainbow did for World War II: It suggestively depicts a mysterious, corrosive evil that is rotting the very fabric of our epistemology and even ontology, masquerading as a historical event that divides time into a before (bad enough) and an after (a whole lot worse, if it even exists at all)." http://www.veteranstoday.com/2015/12/01/pynchons-bleeding-edge-the-great-american-911-truth-novel/
