Micro-credit fuels sex trafficking, human trafficking and organ trafficking

Yunus invented 'micro-credit'--giving loans to very poor people who would not otherwise be eligible for loans. Micro-credit is touted is a great mechanism to lift people out of poverty. In reality, it sinks people further into debt. Borrowers often struggle paying off the debt and turn to loan sharks and are even forced to sell their organs, many times not getting the money promised for an organ. Here is an academic paper entitled “Microcredit-not yet a panacea to end trafficking in women” which sets out the downfalls of micro-credit and how it fuels the sex and human trafficking industry. It speaks specifically about Yunus and Grameen:
*Many borrowers are pushed to take out additional loans from local lenders at high interest rates to meet the initial payments on the microloan.76 They also seek economic opportunities elsewhere and, as a result, have a high risk of being lured into seemingly legitimate employment positions that operate as a guise for trafficking.77 * https://archive.is/VPPyZ
