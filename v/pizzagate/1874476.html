While the vast majority of plea deals are in exchange for not having a trial, if there are several defendants in the case often a deal is made for some to testify against one of the others. The more important a case is, the more likely a plea involves testimony. A drug bust of one person might get a plea deal in exchange for reducing the number of charges or the sentence. An accomplice in a robbery or murder might get a plea deal in exchange for testimony against his partner.
https://www.law.cornell.edu/wex/plea_bargain

In plea bargains, prosecutors usually agree to reduce defendants' punishment. They often accomplish this by reducing the number or severity of the charges against defendants. They might also agree to recommend that defendants receive reduced sentences. Some plea bargains require defendants to do more than simply plead guilty. For example, prosecutors often offer favorable plea bargains to defendants who agree to testify for the state in cases against other defendants.
