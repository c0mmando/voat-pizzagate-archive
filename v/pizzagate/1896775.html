I've been looking into the "Podesta bus" picture posted by NonFBIAnon, and it originated in a Tweet by John Podesta on May 29, 2015, where he is dressed in a red t-shirt picturing woman's dress with a pearl necklace (?). He tweets:

Ready to accessorize? http://hrc.io/1eDlzSw

http://archive.is/GXOAX

It was only after reading the comments that I realized his t-shirt was of a woman's outfit. Initially, I thought it was a pizza deliveryman costume. I wasn't the only one to think so, judging by comments. The link he gives is to the Hillary campaign's web store.

A few days after the Tweet, a fawning Washington Post article came out, describing how the thrifty John Podesta decreed that staffers must take bus rides to commute from NY to Washington. The reporter glowingly details how the humble Podesta stands in line with a duffle bag of laundry and sits with the common people and rarely sees high-powered Washington people on his commutes.

https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-politics/wp/2015/06/09/how-cheap-is-hillary-clintons-campaign-this-cheap/ ?

The $30 bus fare on Vamoose is about a third of the cheapest Amtrak train ticket, and is less than the taxes alone on a coach seat on the NY-DC shuttle plane flight — which is why Clinton campaign manager Robby Mook (who proudly tells staffers and donors alike that he [Podesta] is “really, really cheap,” as in second-hand furniture and no-cab-rides cheap), has decreed that staffers must schlep back and forth between Washington and New York on the bus.

(Interestingly enough, this WaPo article is the origin of the line: "Usually cheerful, Podesta has a profane alter ego nicknamed Skippy you don’t want to meet".)

In the article, Podesta claimed the low-frills commute has built team spirit among his campaign staff. "Ever the leader" John Podesta conceived the idea of posting a cardboard bus in the Brooklyn Clinton HQ with the names of bus-riding staffers as a way to honor them:

A cheap bus ride has become an inside joke and something of a merit badge for Clinton staffers, many of whom commute frequently for meetings and other work in Washington. There is a hand-lettered, bus-shaped honor roll on the wall at the campaign’s Brooklyn headquarters, listing those who have eschewed cushier train or plane rides customary for business travel.

The reporter is careful to note that, although there is a luxury bus available for the NY-Washington commute, "As the most senior staffer, the ascetic Podesta embodies the new campaign’s tightfisted ethos" and

For the record, the fancier Vamoose Gold Bus — with leather seats and bottled water — is off limits at $60 a ticket.

However, a quick look through the Podesta Wikileaks emails reveals quite the contrary. John Podesta takes the luxury Vamoose Gold Bus from Bethesda, MD, to NYC many times. Sample emails below:

https://wikileaks.org/podesta-emails/emailid/16375

http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20100624005063/en/Vamoose-Bus-Launches-Scheduled

Favorable Twitter reactions to the WaPo article from Podesta's friends were collected by his secretary and sent to him in this email:

https://wikileaks.org/podesta-emails/emailid/55807

How this relates to Seth Rich's murder is not clear at this time, although it does reveal that Podesta is disingenuous about taking the economy commute.
