samsg 2016-12-01T08:45:59

Some basic stuff for researching businesses in US:

Search for: "SOS [your state] business lookup" and "DOR [your state] business lookup" and "[your city] business lookup" t. SOS = Secretary of State, DOR = Department of Revenue. This will help you find the State's website to look things up.

If you have an address you can try searching "[your State County] parcel viewer" This can help you find the State website that may list the owner and sales history of a commericial/residential property.

Another good research tool: Public Library System. Go to your library website and login. Look for online databases. Here's an example: http://kcls.org/resources-types/databases/ See all those things on that list? Some of those are expensive paid subscription databases that you get to use for free by using your library card. I assume most public libraries have similar tools.

Also, if your browser can't search by image, download a plugin that lets you right click an image and run an image search.

Another kind of cool tool is google correlate, which tries to correlate search results. Here's an example. https://www.google.com/trends/correlate/search?e=comet+ping+pong&t=all# What's correlated with searching for Comet Ping Pong? 1.) Searching for a ticket to DC 2.) "So Others Might Eat" What's number 2 about? Let's look that up...http://some.org/ oh look at that. A charity in the DC area? Could be nothing, but maybe something to look into.
