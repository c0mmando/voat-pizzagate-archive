On 11/01/13 A Brief of Appellees was filed in the Pennsylvania Supreme Court holding that PA's Sex Offender Registration Act (SORNA) was unconstitutional as it applies to juveniles.

Prior to 2012, children convicted of sex offenses in PA were not required to register as sex offenders. With the adoption of SORNA, PA now requires children adjudicated of certain sexual offenses to register as sex offenders for the rest of their lives. Appellees successfully challenged the constitutionality of SORNA as applied to children.

This case was before the Court as a matter of exclusive jurisdiction on appeal from an Order of the Court of Common Pleas in York County, PA, Juvenile Division. The Trial Court held PA's SORNA statute unconstitutional as applied to children, both retroactively and prosepctively. Appellees were adjuducated delinquent for qualifying offenses when they were children and retroactively registered as sex offenders under SORNA.
