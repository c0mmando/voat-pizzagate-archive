The innocence of Awa Kandaya is only a facade. The virginity of this 20-year-old woman, a schoolgirl's backpack on her shoulders and a charming smile , was snatched from her when she was nine years old in a "sexual initiation" camp in southern Malawi . According to local tradition, a man, paid by his parents to teach him " the things of life ", has violated her. Eleven years later, the parents spoke of the camp. Rape remains a taboo.

Sitting on the steps of a building in Nampugo, a village in the district of Mulanje, miles from the first asphalt road, Awa explains the rite. While it is common to some East African countries that gradually abolish it, this tradition of "sexual initiation" of girls remains very much established in Malawi. Here, as in the majority of rural areas, they must all go to a camp from their first menstruation. They come out if not deflowered, at least profoundly changed.

" Parents enroll their daughters in the camp, it's a family affair," says Esitele Paulo, one of the two organizers of the camp where Awa Kandaya was sent. They show up during the September holidays and are with us for two weeks. " Two weeks to make these little girls" women "before time supposed to be able to take charge of a home.

A real tool of enslavement to men, the initiation camp is run by two women. " We leave the village, in a building away from the men where we are alone with the organizers of the camp," recalls the young Awa. " Once the first rituals begin, we understand that we are here to learn how to please a man, how to please him sexually, " she continues. Becoming anxious to evoke her memories, the girl loses her smile.

Teaching "life"

Nothing appears on the practices of the camp: the omerta is entire. To the gamines who refuse to yield to the rite, the law of the ancestors promises skin diseases and family woes. Some, naive, are happy to go to what they believe is a holiday camp, others have already heard rumors, or NGO denunciation messages. But, willingly or unwillingly, the vast majority of girls resign themselves to it, encouraged by mothers who perpetuate what they themselves have suffered without telling their children what is in store for them.

" They are taken to the river, the girls undress, they must be naked , explains without inconvenience the farmer of the place, barefoot and dressed in his traditional chitenje blue king. Esitele Paulo, co-organizer of one of the sexual initiation camps in the district of Mulanje in southern Malawi. Hyenas are legion in rural areas

Here, tradition takes precedence over individual consent, basic hygiene rules and family planning. Among other laws taught in the camp, everything that relates to menstruation - the use of sanitary napkins or tampons - must be hidden from men because they may be disgusted and no longer want to touch their wives. In contrast, no teaching on the female reproductive system, procreation, any encouragement to the use of contraceptives. There is also no prevention on the transmission of AIDS .

" These camps are a wash brain of those girls who become women too quickly denounced Joyce Mkandawire, the NGO Let's Girls Lead. The consequences are disastrous. And after the camp, many girls get married and leave school. "In Malawi, one girl in two is married before she turns 18. In Mulanje, the weight of tradition is powerful as much as its questioning is slow. " How many have the strength to challenge this culture when your mother, your grandmother tell you it's okay? How many have the strength to say that a " hyena " is a rapist? "
