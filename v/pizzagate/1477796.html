"He met literally everyone several times" is a figurative statement and not literally true.

Also you're employing a logical fallacy called Argument from Incredulity. You're saying you can't imagine that he met these other people without meeting Clinton, therefore he must have met Clinton. That is suspicion and not evidence. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_from_ignorance#Argument_from_incredulity.2FLack_of_imagination

The most famous use of this argument that I'm aware of was when Paul Wolfowitz was attacking the Army's assessment of how many troops would be need in Iraq after the initial fighting.

Wolfowitz: But some of the higher-end predictions that we have been hearing recently, such as the notion that it will take several hundred thousand U.S. troops to provide stability in post-Saddam Iraq, are wildly off the mark. First, it’s hard to conceive that it would take more forces to provide stability in post-Saddam Iraq than it would take to conduct the war itself and to secure the surrender of Saddam’s security forces and his army. Hard to imagine.
