The Williams College Museum of Art is the latest regional institution to receive a major gift of artworks from Peter Norton , the software developer, art collector and philanthropist who donated 75 pieces to the Tang Teaching Museum at Skidmore last November. Among the 68 artworks being donated to Williams are pieces by such contemporary artists as Tracy Emin, Anna Gaskell , Adrian Piper, Allan Ruppersberg, Kathleen Schimert, Nayland Blake and Christopher Woo, many of them newcomers to the museum’s collection. They span the early 1990s to mid-2000s.

All of the pieces being donated comes from Norton’s personal collection. Best known for his anti-virus software, he made his first, flush round of art donations in 2000, when he gave more than 1,000 pieces to 32 institutions.

In this latest round, designed to bolster the visual arts in higher education, he’s also given works to the Berkeley Art Museum and Pacific Film Archive at the University of California, the Rose Art Museum at Brandeis, the Mildred Lane Kemper Art Museum at Washington University and other art museums at colleges and universities around the country.
