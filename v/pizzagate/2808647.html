Former Pima County Justice Court Employee Guilty Of Child Pornography

Daniel Courville, a former Pima County Justice Court employee, was found guilty in federal court of knowing access with intent to view child pornography depicting pre-pubescent minors. Courville faces a sentence of up to 20 years’ imprisonment.

Courville, who formerly worked as computer programmer and specialist for Pima County Justice Court, was indicted by a federal grand jury after an investigation revealed that Courville had searched for, downloaded, and accessed child pornography images and videos over the internet.

Courville’s sentencing is scheduled for Oct. 2018. His imprisonment will be followed by lifetime supervised release, with stringent sex offender conditions, including the condition that he register as a sex offender.
