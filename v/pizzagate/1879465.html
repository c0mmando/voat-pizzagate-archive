I wondered who would approve this, considering the gruesome past associated with this item. While I can understand trying to preserve the original for archaeological purposes, once ISIS destroyed it, let it go. Recreating the arch to celebrate Syria's history seems overboard. Surely there must be something better in Syria's heritage that they could have celebrated. If not, it's not a heritage worth celebrating. Also, how is your country standing up to a barbaric group by placing a symbol from an ancient religion that was as much, if not more barbaric than ISIS? According to the Christianity Today article, there were plans to build a full replica temple in London. Thankfully, those plans were scrapped.
