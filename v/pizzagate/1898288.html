Took a trip down there last year. I've been an annual visitor to the Virgin Islands (US and British) for the last 15 years however this was the first visit with Pizzagate fresh on the brain. First day visited Cafe Roma on St. John. We arrived and were the first ones to sit down when they opened. I first realized something was off when the bartender (late 50s, early 60s, gay), brought up a group of elementary aged children (no parents in sight) and had them all sit up at the bar and was teaching them to fold pizza boxes. I really didn't think anything of it at the time then I noticed the details in the painted wallpaper (see album) Now I know that the 'hurricane hook' is a popular symbol down there, could I have been mistaken?

Well fast forward to my visit to Skinny Legs (best mahi-mahi sandwiches on the island) and saw more interesting art. The most damning thing there was definitely the 'illuminati' eye surrounded by the pedo swirl and LGL double-heart. (see album) Down the street they had a 'masquerade' night for new years eve at a place called The Indigo Grill. That entire side of the island (coral bay) is very shady. I also hiked to the top of Caneel hill and got some great 40 megapixel shots of Jeffery Epstein's island. I don't have the ability to upload those now but the biggest takeaway is more detail on some of the landscaping on the island (locations of planted palm trees, stuff like that). I don't really have the ability or resources to dig into Cafe Roma or Skinny Legs, looking for some feedback on these pics.

Spent a day talking to cab drivers on St. Thomas, all of them know about Jeffrey Epstein but the gist of their attitude towards him was basically "there's tons of rich billionaires around here, we've heard about what he might have did or didn't do but it's none of our business"

Of course I'll be returning later this year, I'll be chartering a boat to drive around Little St. James Island to get better pictures.
