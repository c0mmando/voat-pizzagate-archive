I've checked out dudipary.com and that address seems to be an old Spam Mail address.

But I also tried to find out more about the sender's Domain, "esag.net". Here's what I found:

  • I was unable to connect to www.esag.net , which would probably suggest the address is no longer working

  • I ran a Whois trace on the domain and found consistent results on all three tracking websites: ICANN.org , Whois.net and IP-adress.com . The domain is still reserved, expires in 2017, and the registrant is located in Dresden, Germany .

  • Wikipedia gave me three possibilities:

1) École Suisse d'Archéologie en Grèce

2) École supérieure de design, d'art graphique et d'architecture intérieure (Penninghen school or ESAG Penninghen), a high school in Paris

3) Expression site associated gene , a gene found in the bloodstream expression site of the variable surface glycoproteins of the sleeping sickness parasite, Trypanosoma brucei

  • A StartPage search showed me two relevant hits: esag.ch/ and esag.org/

  • esag.ch/ - Esag Innovative Services seems to be a webpage for a electronics retailer located, according to their contact page , in Waedenswil, Switzerland .

  • http://esag.org/ - European Society of Aesthetic Gynecology

A quick whois trace on all previous websites, ICANN.org and Whois.net shows the registrant is located in Athens, Greece , and IP-adress.com , shows the registrant is located in Waltham, Massachusetts .

All three show the domain was registered in October 2015.

Here's what it says on their About page :

The European Society of Aesthetic Gynecology, E.S.A.G. is a premier professional organization of cosmetic gynecologists located in Europe as well as worldwide, who specialize in aesthetic (cosmetic) surgical gynecology. E.S.A.G. aims to decisively contribute towards the promotion of innovation in aesthetic surgical gynecology in Europe and beyond.

Madeleine McCann disappeared on May 3rd, 2007 .

On the other hand, the fact that the e-mail could have been sent by someone working at the European Society of Aesthetic Gynecology " who specialize in aesthetic (cosmetic) surgical gynecology ", that makes my eyebrows rise a little higher. all the possibilities above point to the e-mail originating from someplace in Europe.

Too farfetched? You tell me. But please consider for a moment that:

If the address in the email body ( http://dudipary.com) ) indicates that the e-mail was the result of automated Spam - and it certainly seems so -, let me remind you that spam can be automated through many variables, some of which are recent search terms in your browser history and Geolocation .

  2. Even if this is nothing but Spam, bare in mind that it was sent by a non-existing esag.net which could have migrated to esag.ch or esag.org. Regardless of its migration, these are all Europe-based domains.
